Acryllic / Collage Examples

These are a few examples of my work in acrylics and collage. I have been working in this medium since 2020.

"...Ahab Stands Alone" (2020)

  • This piece was inspired by the novel "Moby Dick" by Herman Melville. It is my brother's favorite book
    and I wanted to create something that he would appreciate. The piece is acryllic on canvas, and is three
    dimensional in nature. It utilizes driftwood, seashells and the remains of a horseshoe crab found all on
    location in Orleans, MA in September of 2020.

"Crime of the Century" (2019) - First Version

  • This piece was inspired by a screenplay entitled "Jafsie" that is based on the infamous 1932 Lindbergh
    Kidnapping Case that was told from an unorthodox point of view: that of the go-between for the Lindbergh's
    and the kidnappers - a 78 year old school teacher named Dr. John F. Condon (J.F.C). It was the first canvas
    I ever designed. Utilizing all kinds of mixed media, from vellum and acrylic to cardstock. Above is the first
    version from 2019, which was gifted to the co-author of the screenplay.

"Crime of the Century" (2020) - Second Version

  • This is the second version of the piece, which I kept for myself. It is a more refined version of the
    original, and was created in 2020. The piece is intended to evoke media's cavalier attitude and sheer focus
    on bottom line, the nature of the world's first celebrity, and how a child's life got lost in the shuffle.

"Forgive Dallas" (2021)

  • This piece was inspired by the 1963 assassination of President John F. Kennedy, a favorite subject of poet
    Tohm Bakelas, of which this was a commission. It is a collage piece similar to the Lindbergh collage, but it
    pushes further - integrating newspaper clippings, photo paper, vellum, acryllic, 16mm film strips, dye, and
    caro syrup. It even utilizes several vintage Kennedy political buttons. Bellow is the poet proudly displaying
    the finished canvas.

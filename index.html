About Me

My name is Patrick Flynn, and I am a filmmaker, musician and artist working in the media industry
for the last ten years. In that time, I have written and directed documentary features, narrative short
films, and plays for the stage. I have written multiple screenplays in that time period, and have produced
and recorded music for a variety of genres. I have worked as a video editor for NBC-Universal, and
edited narrative feature films and done extensive work as an editor of bluray content for several features.
Since 2020, I have begun work in acrylics and collage-work on canvas. Several of my pieces have been
recomissioned over the years due to demand.

Current Projects

  • The Hard Fall: Envisioned as an eight-episode true crime podcast about the legal odyssey of Jimmy
    and Camille Landano, this project has just been awarded a 2025 grant by The Puffin Foundation. It
    attempts to demystify the complicated judicial process by utilizing legal and law enforcement experts,
    archival interviews, and scripted scenes with actors to achieve a semblance of the truth and to humanize
    the story of Prisoner #59554 in Rahway State Prison: Vincent J. Landano and how his circle of supporters
    became larger (including a disaffected juror and a recanted eyewitness) as Prosecutors, Judges, and the
    entire legal apparatus of the State of New Jersey attempts to silence him, his wife, and his legal team.
